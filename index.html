Shopfloor myth-busting
Myth
"Our production lines are custom. AI won't understand our machines or processes."
Reality
AI understands context, not machines. Feed it your SOP, PID, or batch record and it can answer questions, spot gaps, and help troubleshoot -- the same way a smart colleague would read the manual.
Myth
"I'm not technical enough to use AI tools."
Reality
If you can send a WhatsApp message, you can use AI. Just type what you need in plain language. No coding, no special skills. The more you describe your situation, the better the output.
Myth
"AI will give wrong answers for GMP and regulated processes."
Reality
AI is a draft-and-assist tool, not a decision-maker. Use it to speed up writing, summarizing, and checking. Always have the qualified person verify before sign-off. The accountability stays with you.
Myth
"AI is only for IT or data teams."
Reality
Production engineers, quality managers, HR, supply chain, and shift supervisors are all finding daily time savings. If your job involves writing, reading, deciding, or explaining -- AI can help.

The SAATHI guardrails
01The 80/20 ruleAI drafts the first 80%. You add the final 20% -- the judgment, the polish, the accountability. Never submit AI output without a human review.
02The sanity checkAI can hallucinate facts. Always verify critical outputs -- especially specifications, regulatory values, safety thresholds, or anything that goes into a batch record.
03No confidential dataNever paste customer data, financial forecasts, trade secrets, or proprietary formulations into public AI tools. Use only Gemini via the approved Colgate workspace.
04Human touch lastEmpathy, authority, and accountability cannot be outsourced. AI writes the draft. A qualified human signs off, approves, and owns the outcome.
05Start a habit, not a projectPick one task from these 12 cards today. Spend 10 minutes. Build confidence slowly. AI adoption is a skill, not a one-time event.
